ALL 125 Assignment Preparation Tool

Helping students prepare for the Group Presentation assignment. Navigate through the sections to learn about requirements, tips, and best practices.

Assignment Instructions

Prepare a 10-minute presentation that compares and contrasts the articles you each prepared individually for the Reading Reflection. Each group should focus on a shared theme, drawing connections across your selected articles.

Core Requirements

  • Team size: 3–5 students
  • Length: Maximum 10 minutes
  • Format: Recorded presentation uploaded to ClickUP
  • Content: Demonstrate how your team's personal, cultural, and disciplinary perspectives shape interpretation of articles
  • GenAI Image: Include a GenAI-generated image that captures the collective theme
  • Translanguaging: Accompany the image with one sentence in a primary language spoken by a group member, along with English interpretation

Evaluation Criteria (30 marks total)

  • Interpretation and engagement: 10 marks
  • Personal, cultural, and disciplinary reflection: 8 marks
  • Argumentation and comparison: 4 marks
  • GenAI image and translanguaging: 4 marks
  • Presentation structure and clarity: 4 marks

Submission Guidelines

  • Submit PowerPoint outline of your presentation on ClickUP before the deadline
  • Presentations should be recorded and uploaded, rather than delivered live in class

Reflective Questions

Individual Reflection

Before collaborating as a team, consider:

  • What specific role will you take in the team, and how will you ensure your contribution is equal to others?

Team Reflection Questions

  1. How will your team structure the presentation to cover all evaluation criteria effectively?
  2. How will your team demonstrate how personal, cultural, and disciplinary perspectives shape interpretation of articles?
  3. What strategies will you use to connect your individual articles to the shared theme?

Visual Aid Guide

Effective visuals strengthen your argument. Follow these guidelines:

Relevance

Ensure visuals directly support your key points (e.g., a graph showing unemployment trends to support an argument about economic policies)

Clarity

Avoid clutter: use clear labels, legends, and concise titles. Remove unnecessary elements that don't contribute to your message.

Acknowledgement

If using AI-generated visuals (e.g., from Canva, ChatGPT), cite the tool used (e.g., "Generated using Canva AI") in the slide notes

Example Implementation

A bar chart comparing corruption levels across countries, with a caption: "Data from Transparency International (2023) shows that countries with strong anti-corruption laws (e.g., Botswana) have lower corruption scores, supporting our argument that systemic reforms are effective."

Argument Comparison

Effectively comparing arguments strengthens your presentation. Follow this structure:

Identify Similarities

Find common themes or perspectives across your articles

Highlight Differences

Note how different perspectives approach the same topic

Connect to Theme

Explain how these comparisons contribute to understanding your shared theme

Team Coordination

To ensure equal contribution and effective collaboration:

Best Practices

  • Assign specific roles: research lead, visual designer, scriptwriter, presenter
  • Track progress: use a shared document (e.g., Google Docs) to track contributions and deadlines
  • Regular check-ins: hold team meetings to review progress and address issues
  • Peer evaluation: use evaluation forms to assess each member's input
  • Clear deadlines: set individual milestones for each component of the presentation

Example Workflow

"Each member will present a section of the argument, with the research lead providing evidence for all sections. A shared timeline ensures all tasks are completed before the deadline. Weekly check-ins will review progress and adjust as needed."

Conclusion Tips

A strong conclusion should:

Summarise Key Points

Briefly recap the main arguments without introducing new information

Restate Position

Clearly reiterate your stance on the issue

Memorable Closing

End with a powerful statement or call to action that resonates with your audience

Example Conclusion

"In conclusion, addressing unemployment requires targeted education reforms and entrepreneurship support. As evidenced by our analysis, these strategies not only reduce joblessness but also foster long-term economic resilience. Let us advocate for policy changes that empower communities and drive sustainable growth."

GenAI & Translanguaging

Translanguaging involves strategically using multiple languages to enhance communication. Examples:

GenAI Image Prompt

Create a clear, descriptive prompt for your GenAI image that captures your team's collective theme or response to it

Translanguaging Integration

Include one sentence in a primary language spoken by a group member, along with English interpretation (e.g., "The concept of 'ubuntu' (humanity towards others) underpins our argument for community-driven solutions to corruption")

Key Principle

Ensure translanguaging is purposeful and supports understanding, not just for show. Cite sources where language use is relevant to your argument.
